The Sediment and The Loom

NOBODE

A Media Archaeology of the Generative AI Assemblage

Exploring the layered traces of digital presence (Sediment) woven through the structured processes of computation (Loom). This collection investigates temporality, interaction, and digital being within the complex network of modern generative systems.

Project Collection Overview • MMXXV

12 interactive audiovisual systems exploring time, emergence, and digital being. Experience Cellular Automata, temporal slicing, reactive sonification, and live interaction shaping evolving digital traces.

Themes: Persistence, Identity, Hauntology, Generative Aesthetics, Sovereignty.

01

Nobode

How is presence recorded digitally? Explore identity and sovereignty through temporal slicing, creating visual 'sediment' from live interaction.

02

Perdurant Life Time

Explore entities that persist through time. An interactive audiovisual system where movement births evolving patterns via cellular automata.

03

Oceanic Hauntology

How does the digital past persist? Explore hauntology through oceanic metaphors, interactive waves, and decaying visual echoes.

04

Reflective Currents

How do algorithmic currents reflect us? An interactive visualization exploring energy flows on a virtual loom shaped by your input.

05

Wave Machine

Explore oceanic dynamics computationally. A generative textile where interaction creates propagating waves and persistent visual traces.

06

Coral Threads

Witness emergence inspired by coral growth. A living, sonified textile driven by cellular automata reacting to interaction.

07

Echo Line

Explore temporal feedback and resonance. A reactive textile visualizing past interactions as decaying linear echoes.

08

Echo Zimmer

Interactive system evoking cinematic emotion. A reactive textile generating evolving, atmospheric soundscapes inspired by Hans Zimmer.

09

Harmonic Life

Cellular automata as a music generator. Interact with a grid where evolving patterns create layered harmonic soundscapes.

10

Dead Life

Explore entropy and decay via cellular automata, inspired by the psychedelic, improvisational sound of the Grateful Dead.

11

Harp Life

Directly sonify cellular automata patterns. A grid of virtual harp strings plays emergent harmonies as the system evolves.

12

Miles Life

Generative system evoking Miles Davis's electric period, using cellular automata to create atmospheric textures and modal harmonies.
